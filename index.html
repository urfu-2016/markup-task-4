Royals honour Queen Mother in Charles’s model village

The Queen has un­veiled the latest statue to grace the grounds of the  Prince of Wales’s Pound­bury es­tate: a bronze of the late Queen Eliza­beth the Queen Moth­er.

She was joined by the Duke of Ed­in­burgh, Prince Charles and the Duch­ess of Corn­wall as they took a tour of the newly com­pleted Queen Moth­er Square.

Prince Charles, whose grand­moth­er died aged 101 in 2002, told the crowd who had gathered for the un­veil­ing of the 10ft statue, right:

It is with im­mense pride and in lov­ing memory of my darling grand­moth­er that I open her square.
He ad­ded:
For me one of the most es­sen­tial fea­tures of Pound­bury has been its mixed use and mixed in­come com­pos­i­tion — a dan­ger­ously re­volu­tion­ary move 25 years ago

He also ac­know­ledged that his ar­chi­tec­tur­al views had been out of fa­vour, de­scrib­ing how he had been bat­tling against the tide.

Twenty five years ago all this was a mere dream to most people ex­cept for the late Sir Dav­id Landale, the then Duchy of Corn­wall sec­ret­ary, a com­pletely mad dream.

Bat­tling against the tide is an un­com­fort­able ex­per­i­ence, I can as­sure you. For that reas­on I’m in­debted to the bril­liant mas­ter plan­ner and con­ven­tion­al mould-break­er Léon Kri­er and An­drew Hamilton, the long-suf­fer­ing and highly pro­fes­sion­al de­vel­op­ment dir­ect­or, for help­ing to make the scheme a real­ity.

Prince Charles went on to thank the build­ing com­pan­ies in­volved without whose work none of this would ever have been achieved to such a high stand­ard of qual­ity and their loc­al crafts­men, whom he called Dor­set men through and through.

Work began on the project in 1993 with the idea to cre­ate an urb­an quarter of Dorchester that would give pri­or­ity to people, rather than cars, and where com­mer­cial build­ings were mixed with res­id­en­tial areas, shops and leis­ure fa­cil­it­ies to cre­ate a walk­able com­munity.

Pound­bury is home to 3,000 people in a mix of private and af­ford­able hous­ing. The es­tate also provides jobs for 2,100 people and is home to 185 busi­nesses.

An­drew Hamilton, de­vel­op­ment dir­ect­or for the Duchy of Corn­wall, told Prince Charles:

You, sir, single-handedly set out to chal­lenge the ac­cep­ted or­tho­doxy of plan­ning and de­vel­op­ment in this coun­try. At a time when there is a clam­our for new hous­ing I be­lieve that Pound­bury stands as a beacon to the way for­ward.
